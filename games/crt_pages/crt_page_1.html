The core gameplay is to emulate the experience of “play” an VHS tape on an old CRT. The player is lost in a monochrome maze while a scary monster chases you down. The camera angles are stiff and awkward, to balance it out the players get the power to control time itself. The game also features an AI powered smart console that the players can consult with. An entity living in CRT if you will.

It has undergone two versions, one is the old yellow tone “backrooms” inspired look which I worked on at school; another is the updated stylized monotone version which was added after the class.

Time Manipulation

Got bored during the semester and I made some time manipulation mechanics inspired by Jojo. It fits the theme of watching old VHS on a CRT. PAUSE (time stop) is the most straightforward, but what if you can REWIND and FAST FROWARD too?


Rewind

To achieve rewind, I am storing the following:

  • player position
  • player camera direction
  • enemy position
  • enemy state

They are stored into a list each second. The list is expandable based on how many seconds are allowed to be rewinded. I had to limit the time units on the game to seconds since doing this per-frame is inconsistent and extremely not performative.

There is a cooldown to pervent spamming and also to avoid bugs if player time skips to before the game starts.

Fast Forward Skip

^ The skip happens at 00:06 in the video.

Enemy now has a collider ball attached to the front of it. When that collider ball gets detected by a player raycast, it triggers a time skip. This creates the illusion when:

the enemy is about to enter player's view
-> skips time as if the enemy appeared out of nowhere.

Coupled with changing the clock, it makes a pretty convincing the "King Crimson" effect.

The player's momentum is perserved and basically flashes forward in whichever direction they were going before.

Rewind & skip parameters are adjustable.
